# Physics Basics

Below is the comprehensive guide to passing the **Physics Basics** course, divided into Exercises  and Lectures. It provides detailed information on the grading system, expectations, and workflow for students.

## 1. Exercises (Tutorials/Labs): name C[number]

Passing the exercises is **mandatory**. You must obtain a positive grade in the exercises to be admitted to the Final Exam.

### **Important Rule: No Retakes**

There are **no retake terms** for exercises. The grade is earned continuously throughout the semester. If you fail the exercises, you fail the course immediately.

### **Workflow & Assignments**

* **Rapid Pace:** Assignments begin immediately (e.g., graded tasks as early as Week 3).
* **Preparation vs. Class Performance:** You are expected to solve problems independently before class.
* You may and you are encouraged to use AI tools or external resources to study and understand the material *outside* of class.
* *In Class:* You must present your solution by sharing a screenand verbally describe your methodology. The focus is on **understanding**. If you cannot explain the "how" and "why" of your solution (defend your logic), you will not receive credit.

* **Penalties:** Failure to prepare or inability to solve tasks results in "negative points" (minuses). Accumulating these will prevent you from passing the subject.

### **Grading Method for exercises**

* **Testing:** There will be **2 Colloquia** (tests) during the semester. Each will contribute to your grade. Missing a test or performing poorly will lead to severe negative points leading to failure in exercises.
* Additionally, your performance in class (presentation and understanding) will be evaluated and contribute to your final grade for exercises on equal footing with the tests.
* **Higher Grades:** For students aiming for a grade higher than the basic pass, an **Oral Exam** (verbal questioning) might be available to upgrade the final score of exercises.

## 2. Lecture & Final Exam: name W1

The **Lecture grade** is based on the Final Exam. You must have a passing grade from Exercises to able to take the exam.

### **Exam Option A: "Zeroth Term" (Early Exam)**

* **Format:** Online Test!
* **Timing:** Usually held before end of the semester..
* **Content:** Primarily multiple-choice questions (**ABCD**).

### **Option B: Exam Session (written, in person in-Class)**

* **Format:** Written exam in the physical classroom in the exam session.
* **Restrictions:** **No formula sheets allowed** (closed book). You must know the physics principles by heart.
* **Content:** Moe advanced problems. Do not expect ABCD questions. You will be required to:
* **Prove** theorems.
* **Derive** formulas from scratch.
* Show full logical reasoning.

## 4. Extranet System & Terminology

Explanation of the grading columns used in the university system (e.g., Extranet/Vizja):

* **T1 - Ćwiczenia (Term 1 - exercises):**
* *For Exercises:* The only grade you get. There are no T2/T3 columns for exercises to be filled (it is just artefact of the system). You must pass by having a positive grade (3.0 or higher) in exercises to be admitted to the final exam.
* **T1 - Wykład (Term 1 - lectures):**
* *For Lectures:* This usually represents the result of the "Zeroth Term" or the first scheduled classroom exam in the exam session at the end of June.

* **T2/T3 - Wykład (Term 2/T3 / Retake):**
* Classroom exam held in September. **CRITICAL RULE:** You cannot skip T1 and T2 and later simply "request" T3. The September term is reserved *only* for students who attempted the exam in T2 and failed.
