# 📈 Problem 10 – Infinite Series

We want to determine whether the series

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2}
$$

converges or diverges.

---

# 📌 Step 1: Identify the type of series

This is a **p-series** of the form:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^p}
$$

where:

$$
p = 2
$$

---

# 🔍 Step 2: Use the p-series test

A p-series

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^p}
$$

has the following rule:

- if \(p > 1\), the series **converges**
- if \(p \leq 1\), the series **diverges**

Here:

$$
p = 2 > 1
$$

So the series **converges**.

---

# ✏️ Step 3: Optional note about the exact sum

This is a famous result:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6}
$$

So not only does it converge, but its exact sum is known.

---

# ✅ Final Result

The series

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2}
$$

**converges**.

---

# 💡 Interpretation

As \(n\) becomes very large, the terms

$$
\frac{1}{n^2}
$$

become very small very quickly.  
Because they decrease fast enough, the infinite sum approaches a finite value.

✔ Therefore, the series is convergent.

---

# 🚀 Final Answer

$$
\boxed{\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2}\text{ converges}}
$$

and in fact,

$$
\boxed{\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6}}
$$
