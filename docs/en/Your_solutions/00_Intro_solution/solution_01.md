# Problem 1 – Vector Algebra

## Given

$$
\vec{a} = (2,1,-3)
$$

$$
\vec{b} = (4,-2,1)
$$

---

## a) Magnitude of vectors

Formula:

$$
|\vec{v}| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}
$$

For vector a:

$$
|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{2^2 + 1^2 + (-3)^2}
$$

$$
|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{4 + 1 + 9}
$$

$$
|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{14}
$$

For vector b:

$$
|\vec{b}| = \sqrt{4^2 + (-2)^2 + 1^2}
$$

$$
|\vec{b}| = \sqrt{16 + 4 + 1}
$$

$$
|\vec{b}| = \sqrt{21}
$$

---

## b) Dot product

Formula:

$$
\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} =
a_x b_x + a_y b_y + a_z b_z
$$

$$
= (2)(4) + (1)(-2) + (-3)(1)
$$

$$
= 8 - 2 - 3
$$

$$
= 3
$$

---

## c) Cross product

$$
\vec{a} \times \vec{b} =
\begin{vmatrix}
i & j & k \\
2 & 1 & -3 \\
4 & -2 & 1
\end{vmatrix}
$$

Result:

$$
\vec{a} \times \vec{b} =
(-5,-14,-8)
$$
