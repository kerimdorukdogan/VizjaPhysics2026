# 📐 Problem 5 – Trigonometry

We are given a vector with magnitude **15** and angle **60°** from the horizontal axis.  
We want to find its **horizontal** and **vertical** components.

---

## 📌 Given

- Magnitude of vector:

$$
|\vec{A}| = 15
$$

- Angle with horizontal axis:

$$
\theta = 60^\circ
$$

---

# 🔍 Step 1: Use component formulas

For a vector with magnitude \(A\) and angle \(\theta\):

- Horizontal component:

$$
A_x = A \cos\theta
$$

- Vertical component:

$$
A_y = A \sin\theta
$$

---

# ✏️ Step 2: Calculate the horizontal component

$$
A_x = 15 \cos 60^\circ
$$

We know:

$$
\cos 60^\circ = \frac{1}{2}
$$

So:

$$
A_x = 15 \cdot \frac{1}{2}
$$

$$
A_x = 7.5
$$

---

# 🧩 Step 3: Calculate the vertical component

$$
A_y = 15 \sin 60^\circ
$$

We know:

$$
\sin 60^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

So:

$$
A_y = 15 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

$$
A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

Approximate value:

$$
A_y \approx 12.99
$$

---

# ✅ Final Result

The horizontal component is:

$$
A_x = 7.5
$$

The vertical component is:

$$
A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 12.99
$$

---

# 💡 Interpretation

- The vector points **upward and to the right**
- \(A_x\) tells us how much of the vector is along the **x-axis**
- \(A_y\) tells us how much of the vector is along the **y-axis**

✔ Both components together describe the original vector.

---

# 🚀 Final Answer

$$
\boxed{A_x = 7.5,\quad A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 12.99}
$$
