# ⏱️ Problem 4 – Rearranging Formulas

We are given the formula for the period of a simple pendulum:

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{l}{g}}
$$

Our goal is to rearrange the formula and make **\(g\)** the subject.

---

## 📌 Given formula

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{l}{g}}
$$

Where:

- \(T\) = period
- \(l\) = length of the pendulum
- \(g\) = acceleration due to gravity

---

# 🔍 Step 1: Divide both sides by \(2\pi\)

$$
\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{l}{g}}
$$

This removes the coefficient in front of the square root.

---

# ✏️ Step 2: Square both sides

To remove the square root, square both sides:

$$
\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2 = \frac{l}{g}
$$

---

# 🔄 Step 3: Rewrite the left side

$$
\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{l}{g}
$$

---

# 🧩 Step 4: Solve for \(g\)

Multiply both sides by \(g\):

$$
g \cdot \frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = l
$$

Now divide both sides by \(\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2}\):

$$
g = \frac{l}{T^2/(4\pi^2)}
$$

Dividing by a fraction means multiplying by its reciprocal:

$$
g = l \cdot \frac{4\pi^2}{T^2}
$$

---

# ✅ Final Result

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 l}{T^2}
$$

🎯 This is the formula for **acceleration due to gravity**.

---

# 💡 Quick check

The original formula was:

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{l}{g}}
$$

After rearranging, we found:

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 l}{T^2}
$$

✔ This is the standard pendulum formula solved for \(g\).

---

# 🚀 Final Answer

$$
\boxed{g = \frac{4\pi^2 l}{T^2}}
$$
