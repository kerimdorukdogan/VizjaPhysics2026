# ∫ Problem 8 – Definite Integrals

We want to calculate the area under the curve:

$$
f(x) = \sin(x)
$$

from:

$$
x = 0 \quad \text{to} \quad x = \pi
$$

---

# 📌 Step 1: Write the definite integral

The required area is:

$$
\int_0^\pi \sin(x)\,dx
$$

---

# 🔍 Step 2: Find the antiderivative

The antiderivative of \(\sin(x)\) is:

$$
\int \sin(x)\,dx = -\cos(x)
$$

So:

$$
\int_0^\pi \sin(x)\,dx = \left[-\cos(x)\right]_0^\pi
$$

---

# ✏️ Step 3: Evaluate at the limits

Substitute \(x = \pi\) and \(x = 0\):

$$
-\cos(\pi) - \big(-\cos(0)\big)
$$

We know:

$$
\cos(\pi) = -1
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\cos(0) = 1
$$

So:

$$
-(-1) - (-1)
$$

$$
= 1 + 1
$$

$$
= 2
$$

---

# ✅ Final Result

The area under the curve is:

$$
2
$$

---

# 💡 Interpretation

The graph of \(\sin(x)\) from \(0\) to \(\pi\) stays above the x-axis, so the definite integral directly gives the area.

✔ Therefore, the total area is:

$$
2
$$

---

# 🚀 Final Answer

$$
\boxed{2}
