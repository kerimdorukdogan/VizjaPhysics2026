# 📊 Problem 6 – Function Analysis

We are given the function:

$$
f(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 7
$$

Our goal is to determine whether the function has a **local maximum or minimum** and find its location.

---

# 📌 Step 1: Compute the derivative

To find extrema, we calculate the **first derivative**.

$$
f'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}(3x^2 - 12x + 7)
$$

$$
f'(x) = 6x - 12
$$

---

# 🔍 Step 2: Find critical points

Critical points occur when:

$$
f'(x) = 0
$$

So we solve:

$$
6x - 12 = 0
$$

$$
6x = 12
$$

$$
x = 2
$$

🎯 The critical point is at:

$$
x = 2
$$

---

# ✏️ Step 3: Determine if it is max or min

Look at the **second derivative**:

$$
f''(x) = 6
$$

Since:

$$
f''(x) > 0
$$

the function is **concave upward**.

✔ Therefore the point is a **local minimum**.

---

# 🧮 Step 4: Find the value of the function

Substitute \(x = 2\) into the original function.

$$
f(2) = 3(2)^2 - 12(2) + 7
$$

$$
f(2) = 12 - 24 + 7
$$

$$
f(2) = -5
$$

---

# ✅ Final Result

The function has a **local minimum** at:

$$
x = 2
$$

with value:

$$
f(2) = -5
$$

---

# 🚀 Final Answer

The minimum point is:

$$
(2, -5)
$$

📉 The function decreases until \(x=2\) and increases afterward.
