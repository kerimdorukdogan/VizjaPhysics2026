# ⚖️ Problem 9 – Momentum Comparison

We compare the momentum of:

- a **2-gram fly** moving at **10 m/s**
- a **60-gram tennis ball** moving at **1 m/s**

We use the momentum formula:

$$
p = mv
$$

---

# 📌 Step 1: Convert masses to kilograms

For the fly:

$$
2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}
$$

For the tennis ball:

$$
60 \text{ g} = 0.060 \text{ kg}
$$

---

# 🪰 Step 2: Compute the fly's momentum

$$
p_{\text{fly}} = mv
$$

$$
p_{\text{fly}} = 0.002 \cdot 10
$$

$$
p_{\text{fly}} = 0.020 \text{ kg·m/s}
$$

---

# 🎾 Step 3: Compute the tennis ball's momentum

$$
p_{\text{ball}} = mv
$$

$$
p_{\text{ball}} = 0.060 \cdot 1
$$

$$
p_{\text{ball}} = 0.060 \text{ kg·m/s}
$$

---

# 🔍 Step 4: Compare the two values

Fly:

$$
p_{\text{fly}} = 0.020 \text{ kg·m/s}
$$

Tennis ball:

$$
p_{\text{ball}} = 0.060 \text{ kg·m/s}
$$

Since:

$$
0.060 > 0.020
$$

the tennis ball has the greater momentum.

Also:

$$
\frac{0.060}{0.020} = 3
$$

So the tennis ball's momentum is **3 times larger**.

---

# ✅ Final Result

The **tennis ball** has the greater momentum.

$$
\boxed{p_{\text{fly}} = 0.020 \text{ kg·m/s}}
$$

$$
\boxed{p_{\text{ball}} = 0.060 \text{ kg·m/s}}
$$

---

# 🚀 Final Answer

$$
\boxed{\text{The tennis ball has greater momentum}}
$$

and its momentum is:

$$
\boxed{3 \text{ times larger than the fly's momentum}}
$$
