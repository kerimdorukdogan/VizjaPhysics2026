# 🌍 Problem 8 – Circular Motion

We want to calculate the centripetal acceleration of a person standing on the Earth's equator.

The Earth's radius is approximately:

$$
R = 6378 \text{ km}
$$

---

# 📌 Step 1: Convert the radius to meters

$$
R = 6378 \times 10^3 \text{ m}
$$

$$
R = 6.378 \times 10^6 \text{ m}
$$

---

# ⏱️ Step 2: Use the period of Earth's rotation

A person on the equator makes one full rotation with the Earth in:

$$
T = 24 \text{ h} = 86400 \text{ s}
$$

---

# 🔍 Step 3: Write the formula for centripetal acceleration

For circular motion:

$$
a_c = \frac{v^2}{R}
$$

A very convenient equivalent formula is:

$$
a_c = \omega^2 R
$$

where angular velocity is:

$$
\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T}
$$

So:

$$
a_c = \left(\frac{2\pi}{T}\right)^2 R
$$

---

# ✏️ Step 4: Substitute the numbers

$$
a_c = \left(\frac{2\pi}{86400}\right)^2 (6.378 \times 10^6)
$$

First compute:

$$
\frac{2\pi}{86400} \approx 7.27 \times 10^{-5} \text{ rad/s}
$$

Now square it:

$$
\omega^2 \approx 5.29 \times 10^{-9}
$$

Now multiply by \(R\):

$$
a_c \approx (5.29 \times 10^{-9})(6.378 \times 10^6)
$$

$$
a_c \approx 0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

---

# ✅ Final Result

The centripetal acceleration at the Earth's equator is approximately:

$$
\boxed{a_c \approx 0.034 \text{ m/s}^2}
$$

---

# 💡 Interpretation

This value is much smaller than the usual gravitational acceleration:

$$
g \approx 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

So the Earth's rotation gives only a small centripetal acceleration compared with gravity.

✔ That is why we do not strongly feel this effect in daily life.
